What is the interest coverage ratio?
the interest coverage ratio is a measurement of a compay's ability to pay it's debt payment's. It is sometimes called the times interest earned (TIE) ratio.
How to calculate interest coverage ratio?
To calculate interest coverage ratio you should use the following formula:
interest coverage ratio = earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT) / interest expens
example: company x's earnings in a specific quarter are $837,000 the company also has a debt and it should pay $39,000 every month to pay the total debt.
first we should convert the monthly interest payments into quarterly payments and we can do that by multiplying the monthly payments by three.
quarterly payments = $39,000 * 3
quarterly payments = $117,000
now let's calculate the interest coverage ratio:
interest coverage ratio = earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT) / interest expens
interest coverage ratio = $837,000 / $117,000
interest coverage ratio = 7.15
Why use interest coverage ratio?
the interest coverage ratio is used to find out how easily a company can pay interest on it's outstanding debt. Lenders and investors usually use it to determine a company's riskiness relative to it's current debt or for future borrowing.
